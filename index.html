📋 Overview

This project builds a full financial model in Python to simulate and forecast business performance across multiple scenarios. Starting from revenue and cost drivers, the model projects income statements, operating cash flow, and key business metrics — then stress-tests those projections across different economic assumptions.

Rather than producing a single forecast, the multi-scenario approach shows a range of plausible outcomes — giving a clearer picture of upside potential and downside risk under different conditions.

⚙ Methodology
01
Define Revenue Drivers
Model revenue as a function of growth rates, volume, and pricing assumptions — parameterized per scenario.
02
Build Expense Model
Separate fixed from variable costs. Link variable expenses to revenue and operational volume assumptions.
03
Project Income Statement
Generate a multi-year P&L for each scenario — revenue, gross profit, EBITDA, and net income.
04
Run Scenario Comparison
Output all three scenarios side by side with variance vs. base case highlighted at each line item.
05
Budget Variance Analysis
Compare actuals vs. forecast, decompose variances by driver, and flag items exceeding materiality thresholds.
